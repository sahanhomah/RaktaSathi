Eligibility to Donate Blood

Donating blood is a noble act — but it's also a responsibility. Before donating, it's important to know whether you meet the basic health and safety criteria to ensure both donor and patient safety.

Who Can Donate Blood?

To become a blood donor, you should:

  • 🩸 Be 18 to 65 years old
  • 🩸 Weight at least 50 kg
  • 🩸 Have a hemoglobin level of 12.5 g/dL or more
  • 🩸 Be in good physical and mental health
  • 🩸 Not have donated blood in the last 3 months (for men) or 4 months (for women)
  • 🩸 Be free from infections such as HIV, Hepatitis B/C, or malaria
